Psych on Ice (ice baby)

Partnered with the National Space and Biomedical Research Institute


Individuals face many challenges during long duration space flight that impact their psychological well-being. Confinement, under- or over- work, sleep loss, monotony, and other factors increase stress, and can lead to interpersonal tensions and depression. Conflicts can arise between astronauts and ground control, which could lead to a loss of trust and teamwork. Disputes between crewmembers can destroy team functioning and lead to errors or lack of situational awareness. In the past, many psychological and interpersonal events have happened both in space and Antarctica, which greatly affected the missions.

This project is part of the Autonomous Behavioral Health Countermeasures for Spaceflight project, which has three components: “improving the conflict management content, incorporating an integrated behavioral health assessment, and developing an immersive virtual reality component.” We focus on the the first three aspects, aiming to help reduce stress users through video modules that teach techniques to deal with conflict, stress, and depression.
